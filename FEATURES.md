# Potential Features — Hackathon Roadmap

## High Impact, Moderate Effort

### 1. Real Satellite Data Overlay
Fetch actual satellite positions from CelesTrak/Space-Track TLE data. Show "9,000+ tracked objects right now" with real orbital shells. Judges love real-world data.

### 2. Click-to-Destroy Scenario Mode
Let users click on a satellite to trigger its destruction and watch the cascade unfold in real time. Makes it interactive and visceral — way more compelling than auto-random collisions.

### 3. Narrated Intro / Guided Tour
A 15-second animated flythrough that explains Kessler Syndrome with text overlays ("In 2024, there are 9,000+ active satellites..."). Judges often have 2 minutes — hook them instantly.

## High Impact, Lower Effort

### 4. Danger Heatmap on Earth
Project a color overlay onto the Earth showing which regions sit under the densest debris fields. Makes the "so what?" tangible — "GPS over North America would fail."

### 5. Historical Event Replay
Add presets for real events: the 2009 Iridium-Cosmos collision, China's 2007 ASAT test. Use real parameters. "This actually happened" moments stick with judges.

### 6. Sound Design
Subtle ambient hum, alarm escalation as status goes unstable → runaway, impact sounds on collision. Audio is the most underused sense at hackathons and immediately elevates polish.

## Medium Impact, Quick Wins

### 7. Satellite Count Comparison Bar
Small HUD element: "Starlink plans 42,000 satellites" vs your current count. Context makes the simulation scary.

### 8. Export / Share Snapshot
"Share this cascade" button that screenshots the current state. Viral potential, judges notice shareability.

### 9. Mission Control CRT Theme
A "NASA mission control" green-on-black CRT scanline mode. Pure aesthetic flex.

---

**Recommended combo for max judge impact:** #2 (click-to-destroy) + #6 (sound design) + #3 (narrated intro). Together they turn a visualization into an *experience*.
